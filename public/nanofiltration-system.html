Nanofiltration System

Nanofiltration is a filtration technology that uses porous films or materials with pore sizes between those of Ultrafiltration and Reverse Osmosis. Additionally, due to its broader membrane hole structure compared to Reverse Osmosis membranes, Nanofiltration is sometimes referred to as “loose” Reverse Osmosis and offers improved ion selectivity vs. Reverse Osmosis membranes, which reject all ion species in a feed stream. Nanofiltration membranes are intended to enable monovalent ions to flow through while preventing more complicated structural molecules from passing through and these membranes typically operate between pressures of 120 to 600 psi. These are commonly used for concentrating APIs, antibiotics, peptides, and other compounds with molecular weights larger than 1000 daltons.

Sanitech Engineers has a strong reputation for producing cuttingedge nanofiltration systems that are used in wide variety of industries such as Food and Beverage, Pharmaceuticals, Chemical Processing, Oil and Gas and Textile.
