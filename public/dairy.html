Dairy

The dairy industry is under constant pressure to meet the growing demands of milk and milk products in the urbanized population. India is one of the largest producers of milk and propelling itself toward becoming the number one milk producer in the world. Sanitech Engineers has been providing innovative solutions to meet the unique requirements of the dairy industry for several years. Our latest technology and systems have been specifically designed keeping in mind the growing requirements of the dairy industry, focusing on increased production while bringing down operating costs.

Decalcification

We offer advanced Ion Exchange technology which is widely used by the leading dairy manufacturers for the demineralization and de-acidification of sugar solutions, sweeteners, whey, and organic products. Our systems have been designed to maximize your process performance and improve your product quality.

CIP

Sanitech’s flow plates have been carefully designed to reduce the number of valves and piping to significantly bring down the redundant cost. Our CIP systems comply with the stringent hygiene standards of the dairy industry and guarantee reliable and consistent cleaning. As our CIP systems are automated, they thoroughly clean the interior surfaces of plant equipment or pipeline circuits without dismantling the equipment.

Whey demineralization

We offer high-performance Membrane & ion exchange technologies for whey demineralization process, where whey (cheese, casein, acid whey) is transformed into value-added products like D90 (90% demineralized whey powder) & D70 (70% demineralized). Our processes guarantee higher productivity and better recovery yield than traditional processes.

Whey permeate / Lactose concentration

Our world-class nanofiltration membrane systems offer complete/partial concentration of whey permeate streams to create additional capacity to handle whey at WPC / Ion Exchange / Evaporator houses. Sanitech Engineers’ advanced technologies bring significant value over traditional processes and help reduce the energy load on evaporators too.

Lactose recovery from Mother Liquor

Every lactose line suffers from left over mother liquor leading to a 35-40% loss of lactose in the mother liquor. Our high-performance chromatographic and membrane technology has been designed to address this challenge faced by dairy manufacturers in an effective way and help them prevent losing value in the whey plant.
