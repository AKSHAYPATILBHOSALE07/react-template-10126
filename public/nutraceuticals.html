Oligosaccharides Purification

Oligosaccharides are a combination of monosaccharides that can be purified using ion exchange and chromatography.

Fructo-oligosaccharides (FOS) consist of a short chain of fructose molecules while Galacto-oligosaccharides (GOS) consists of a short chain of Galactose molecules. The separation/purification of these can be done using Ion exchange and nanofiltration techniques.

Sugar

For syrup decolorization in the sugar industry, the ion exchange method is used for color removal. In this process, the resin is used as an ion exchange medium to achieve decolorization. This is achieved by retaining the color on top of the beads or resin. Nanofiltration is also used to decolorize sugar syrup.
